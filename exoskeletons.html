I Keep My Exoskeletons to Myself

by Marisa Crane

Introduction

This novel offers a fresh and engaging perspective on privacy and personal identity. The novel’s cover, featuring a striking illustration of an armored figure in an urban setting, sets the stage for the imaginative narrative within.

Plot and Writing Techniques

The story follows the protagonist, who lives in a world where individuals wear literal exoskeletons to protect their private selves from the invasive scrutiny of others. This unique premise explores how our physical and emotional boundaries shape our interactions and sense of self. The protagonist's journey through a society obsessed with surveillance and control provides a thought-provoking look at the balance between privacy and openness.

Character Believability

Crane’s writing brings to life the tension between personal space and societal expectations. For instance, the protagonist’s struggle to maintain autonomy while navigating the pressures of a highly monitored world is both relatable and compelling. The author writes:

“In a world where every glance is a potential invasion, I’ve learned to guard my space with the same vigilance as I guard my secrets.”

This quote captures the essence of the protagonist’s challenge and underscores the novel’s central theme.

Another notable aspect is Crane’s use of vivid imagery to convey the protagonist’s internal conflicts. The line,

“My exoskeleton is more than just a shield; it’s my sanctuary,”

emphasizes how the protagonist’s physical protection becomes a metaphor for emotional safety. This imagery adds depth to the narrative and helps readers connect with the protagonist’s experiences.

The supporting characters also add richness to the story, each reflecting different aspects of the societal obsession with privacy. The protagonist’s interactions with these characters reveal the complexities of living in a world where personal boundaries are constantly tested.

Conclusion

Overall, I Keep My Exoskeletons to Myself is a thought-provoking and enjoyable read that blends imaginative world-building with meaningful exploration of privacy and self-identity.

Rating: 4 stars

Crane’s unique concept and engaging narrative make this novel a standout. For those interested in a fresh take on these themes, this book is worth picking up.
